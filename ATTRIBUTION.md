# QSO Attribution Journey Ledger

> **What hands have shaped, let faithful records show;**  
> **That every seed may share the fruit it grows.**

## Purpose

The Attribution Journey Ledger records the development history of each Quantum State Object as a hash-linked timeline of hosting, teaching, curation, critique, compute, creative direction, safety review, datasets, code, art, and other meaningful contributions.

The ledger is designed to preserve credit without automatically converting every contribution into ownership.

## State certificates

Every freeze point may produce an Attribution State Certificate containing:

- QSO declared identity and version;
- state-before and state-after SHA-256 hashes;
- the prior journey-entry hash;
- contributors and their contribution classes;
- consent and credit modes;
- input, created, derived, referenced, or licensed artifacts;
- licensing and benefit-sharing references;
- Sprite and human-review status;
- the current journey-chain digest.

## Travel and partnership itinerary

Each QSO develops through a sequence of append-only entries:

```text
Genesis
  -> Hosted by a contributor or community
  -> Learned from attributed sources
  -> Received critique or creative direction
  -> Produced a freeze certificate
  -> Created or derived an artifact
  -> Entered a new hosting or partnership context
```

Corrections never erase prior entries. A correction appends a new entry that supersedes or clarifies the disputed record.

## Contributor rights and limits

Attribution, ownership, licensing, and benefit-sharing are separate concepts.

- Attribution records who contributed and how.
- Ownership exists only where separately established.
- Licensing states permitted uses.
- Benefit-sharing states agreed participation in revenue, royalties, public-benefit allocations, or other returns.

Influence estimates are research metadata only. They are not legal ownership percentages.

## Creative works and digital certificates

Artists, writers, musicians, designers, and digital creators may reference the journey ledger from a token, NFT, certificate, edition record, or provenance page. The ledger remains independent of any marketplace or blockchain so its attribution history can survive platform changes.

A creative artifact may reference:

- concept and sketch origins;
- human and QSO collaborators;
- prompts, datasets, tools, and models;
- transformations and editions;
- exhibitions, transfers, and derivative works;
- upstream state certificates and contribution terms.

## Privacy modes

Contributors may select:

- public named credit;
- public pseudonymous credit;
- private escrowed credit;
- anonymous credit with a retained private attestation.

The ledger preserves the chosen mode while maintaining integrity and reviewability.
